Practical BDD Go See Tips

This interactive talk is for LeSS managers seeking to enhance their organization’s development capabilities and policies. Large-scale Scrum adoptions often involve messy, outdated codebases, making it difficult to introduce modern practices. Join this session to gain practical “Go See” tips on stepping out of the office to observe the code base and test automation at the gemba. I’ll share my experience introducing Behavioral Driven Development (BDD) during LeSS adoptions and how analyzing Gherkin syntax can reveal the state of BDD practices in your organization. Learn how to craft clear, effective Gherkin scenarios that serve as living documentation, strengthening collaboration between business and development teams. Discover core BDD concepts, how Example Mapping improves requirements discovery, and best practices for implementing BDD in remote environments. Walk away with actionable strategies to reduce slow feedback loops, prevent misunderstandings, minimize rework, and accelerate delivery. See how modern practices can drive agile engineering and improve product outcomes.

Inverse Conway Maneuver Myth

In this talk, we will critically examine some popular ideas circulating in top management circles that may actually hinder organizational agility. Through a thought-provoking lens, we will explore why these concepts might not be as effective as they seem and provide compelling arguments to challenge them. Topics we will explore include: -Conway’s Law & The Inverse Conway Maneuver -Cognitive Load & Team Cognitive Load -Code Ownership & Team Ownership -“Having to fix someone else’s shitty code at 2am!” Blazej will challenge the current trends in organizational design, encouraging the audience to rethink “modern” approaches. Using interactive mobile polling, he will engage participants in real-time, offering insights into better organizational design patterns that can boost agility (especially in the age of rapidly advancing GenAI tools for software development). Prepare for fresh perspectives on organizational design topics and strategies to improve your company’s adaptiveness!

Beyond Scrum with AI

AI is rapidly reshaping modern software development, and as we move into the late adoption phase of AI tools, we are gaining a clearer view of its impact. How should organizations adapt to what’s coming next? Join Scrum Barcelona for an insightful discussion at "Beyond Scrum with AI" with Blazej Drobniuch, Pawel Brodzinski and Alex Ballarin. In this talk, Błażej invited Paweł and Alex to a challenge—to discuss the intersection of Scrum and AI. However, bear in mind that this could lead to anything but "How to be a Scrum Master in an AI-powered world." Should we reinvent everything, or does nothing really change? Blazej, an Agile Organizational Consultant, specializes in bridging the gap between business and technical teams. Pawel, a CEO who humorously calls himself the "Chief Cook and Bottle-Washer," brings a unique leadership perspective. Alex, an OKR and Scrum coach and trainer, helping organizations to deploy better their strategies using Agile and OKR.
